Sambar

Sambar is a traditional South Indian lentil stew made with toor dal (pigeon pea lentils), tamarind, and a medley of vegetables, flavored with a fragrant spice blend (sambar powder) and tempered with mustard seeds, curry leaves, and dried red chilies, typically served with rice, Idli, Dosa or Medu Vada for a comforting and filling meal.

  • Ingredients

    • Lentil (Dal)- Forms the base of sambhar. Made with toor dal (split pigeon peas lentil) but can also be made by mixing masoor (red lentils) and toor dal.
    • Vegetables- Tomato, drumstick, eggplant and carrots. (Using different vegetables makes the sambhar flavorful but you do not necessarily need lots of veggies to make delicious sambar.)
    • Sambhar Powder- The taste of the sambar depends heavily on the sambar powder which is a spice mix made by grinding several spices. Use your favorite brand of sambar powder or make it at home.
    • Tamarind & Jaggery- These ingredients add the little tartness & sweetness to the sambar respectively.
    • Final Tadka- A good tempering at the end with mustard seeds, curry leaves and asafoetida makes the sambar very delicious! We will use ghee for tadka in this sambar recipe. (Oil can be used if you wish to keep the recipe vegan.)

  • Steps

    1. Cook the dal with turmeric and salt. You can do this on a stovetop pressure cooker or in Instant Pot.
    2. Soak the tamarind in hot water to extract the tamarind pulp for 20 minutes
    3. Then strain using a strainer, And your tamarind pulp is ready
    4. For the sambar, chop the eggplant into medium cubes and start sauteing the eggplant.
    5. Add your chopped into medium cubes vegetables to the eggplant and cook for 2-3 minutes.
    6. Add water according to the consistency preferred along with sambar powder to the vegetable mix.
    7. Add the tamarind and jaggery pulp.
    8. Add and stir in the cooked dal.
    9. Mix everything well and then let the sambar simmer on medium heat
    10. Make the tadka by sauteing mustard seeds and curry leaves with a small amount of ghee in a small pan.
    11. Pour over the tadka on your cooked sambar and serve hot.

Your sambar is now ready to be served with rice, idli, dosa or other delicacies.
